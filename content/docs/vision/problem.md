# Problem Statement

## 1. Core Problem

Current digital systems are built around platforms that control:

- user data  
- user identity  
- user interactions  

This creates structural dependency of users and organizations on specific platforms and limits their ability to operate independently.

---

## 2. Data Ownership Problem

In existing systems:

- user data is stored inside platforms  
- access to data is controlled by platforms  
- data is duplicated across multiple services  
- users cannot reliably move their data between services  

As a result:

- data silos are formed  
- data consistency is lost  
- users lose control over their own information  

---

## 3. Platform Lock-in

Platforms act as gatekeepers:

- users cannot switch services without losing data and history  
- social graphs and interactions are locked inside platforms  
- platforms can block, restrict, or manipulate user access  

This leads to:

- reduced competition  
- dependency on platform policies  
- inability to build truly independent services  

---

## 4. Fragmented Identity

Identity is:

- platform-specific  
- duplicated across services  
- weakly verifiable  
- трудно переносима между системами  

Users maintain multiple identities:

- different accounts  
- different credentials  
- inconsistent representations  

This prevents:

- reliable trust  
- cross-platform interactions  
- long-term identity continuity  

---

## 5. Lack of Interoperability

Services interact via:

- proprietary APIs  
- bilateral integrations  
- inconsistent data models  

This creates:

- high integration cost  
- fragile connections between systems  
- limited ability to scale ecosystem  

Adding a new service requires:

- multiple integrations  
- data duplication  
- schema translation at each connection  

---

## 6. Central Points of Control

Even systems that claim decentralization often rely on:

- centralized registries  
- certification authorities  
- controlled onboarding of services  
- trusted intermediaries  

These components:

- can become single points of failure  
- can enforce control over access and participation  
- contradict the goal of user independence  

---

## 7. Inconsistent Trust and Security Models

Current systems lack a unified approach to:

- verifying actions  
- tracking responsibility  
- ensuring data integrity across services  

Problems include:

- unclear trust boundaries  
- reliance on platform-level enforcement  
- limited auditability across systems  

---

## 8. Data Duplication and Synchronization Issues

Because data is copied across systems:

- multiple versions of the same data exist  
- synchronization becomes complex and unreliable  
- conflicts and inconsistencies arise  

This leads to:

- outdated or conflicting information  
- increased system complexity  
- higher infrastructure costs  

---

## 9. Limited User Control

Users cannot:

- precisely control access to individual pieces of data  
- delegate access in a consistent way  
- verify how their data is used across services  

Control is coarse-grained and platform-dependent.


---

## 10. Summary

The fundamental problem is:

There is no infrastructure that allows independent services to operate on user-controlled data and identity without introducing new forms of centralization, lock-in, or complexity.

A new system is required that:

- separates data from platforms  
- provides persistent and portable identity  
- enables interoperability without tight coupling  
- minimizes and makes explicit all control points  
- ensures verifiable and consistent interactions across services
